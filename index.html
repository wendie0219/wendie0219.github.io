About

Jiwen Zhang

Hi, I’m Jiwen Zhang, an undergraduate student majoring in English Linguistics at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. My research interests lie at the intersection of linguistics, psychology, and cognitive science, where I leverage computational methods to probe how language is represented and processed in the human brain.

Focus

Research

Current work centers on computational approaches to language learning and cognition, with an emphasis on how linguistic knowledge is structured, acquired, and processed.

Primary Direction

Computer-Assisted Language Learning

I am particularly interested in combining linguistic theory with computational tools to better understand learning processes and build research workflows that connect language data with cognitive interpretation.

Approach

Interdisciplinary by design

My work moves across linguistics, psychology, and cognitive science, aiming to ask precise questions about representation and processing while using quantitative and computational methods to support them.
